# Exodraft Customer Research — Key Findings

**Source:** Danish Technological Institute (Teknologisk Institut)
**Project:** "Future Fans" — Market Analysis for Exodraft
**Date:** July–August 2024
**Method:** 18 semi-structured interviews — 9 dealers + 9 end-users

---

## The Three Key Conclusions (from the report itself)

1. **"It's a DIY project"** — Chimney fans must be so easy to install and service that anyone could walk in off the street and do it.
2. **"A difficult choice made easy"** — Most wood stove owners are certain they don't need a fan. Once they realize they do, choosing one must be effortless.
3. **"Intelligent control"** — Address complexity with a simple, intelligent control system. One control for all motors, as easy as possible.

---

## Why People Don't Have a Chimney Fan (Barriers)

1. **Lack of awareness** — Many simply don't know chimney fans exist
2. **Perceived unnecessity** — Their chimney "works fine" — they don't see the problem
3. **Cost** — High price without clear ROI calculation

---

## Why People Buy a Chimney Fan (Dealer Perspective)

1. Better draft and combustion (hotter fire, less soot on glass)
2. Legal/compliance requirements
3. Better indoor air quality
4. Easier ignition (especially cold chimney starts)
5. Reduce neighbor complaints about smoke
6. Consistent performance in adverse weather

---

## What End-Users Want (After Being Introduced to the Product)

Top 5 value propositions that resonated:
1. **Improved indoor air quality** (top priority)
2. **Reduced soot / cleaner glass doors**
3. **Better combustion and fuel efficiency**
4. **Less pollution / environmental benefits**
5. **Return on investment** — "show me the math"

Key hesitations:
- "My current system is fine"
- Price must be justified by concrete savings/benefits

---

## Feature Priority Ranking

### Tier 1 (Most Important)
- **Noise level** (score: 9.6/10) — Deal-breaker if noisy. Customers expect virtually silent operation. Ruining the cozy atmosphere = product failure.
- **Control / ease of use** (score: 8.6/10) — Simple and intuitive. One control for everything.

### Tier 2
- **Easy to install** (7.7/10) — Self-install is the goal
- **Noise reduction** (7.6/10)
- **Easy to service** (7.6/10) — Important for sweep visits AND self-service
- **Off-grid capability** (7.3/10) — Safety/reliability + energy independence. Solar preferred over battery.

### Tier 3
- **Price** (6.6/10)
- **Made in Denmark** (7.0/10) — Quality/trust signal, but not universal
- **Design/appearance** (5.7/10)

### Tier 4
- **CO2 footprint** (mentioned but lower priority for purchase decision)

---

## Key Insight: Noise is #1

> *"Excessive noise can be a deal-breaker."*
> *"Customer expectations = no noise."*
> *"A noisy fan can ruin the relaxing and cozy atmosphere."*

This is the most important product insight from the research. **Silence is not a feature — it is a prerequisite.**

---

## Future Fan Characteristics (What Customers Want in the Future)

1. **Minimalist, less visible design** — less obtrusive on the chimney
2. **More automation** — self-starting/stopping, auto speed adjustment
3. **Smart home integration** — wireless control, no electrician for install
4. **Environmental features** — particle filters integrated, solar power
5. **Fewer models** — current lineup too complex; smart controls + one platform = multiple use cases covered

---

## Market Context

- **Wood stove usage:** Primarily secondary heating in urban areas (comfort/ambiance). Primary heat in rural areas.
- **Germany:** Market appears stagnant (report notes)
- **Austria:** Government incentives driving shift from oil to wood — growth market
- **Environmental awareness growing:** Dry wood, proper ventilation becoming more important to users

---

## Dealer Insights on Product Line

Strong preference for **fewer models with smarter controls** rather than many specialized models. Current variety is confusing for both customers and dealers.

> *"A single model could cater to multiple needs if equipped with advanced control systems that allow for customization."*
