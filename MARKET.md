## Growth Drivers

### 1. Heat Pump Transition
As gas/oil boilers are replaced by heat pumps, wood-burning stoves transition from "secondary" to "primary" or "backup" heat source. Draft problems that were tolerated become unacceptable.

### 2. EU Air Quality Regulation
- **Ecodesign 2022 for Solid Fuel Local Space Heaters** — efficiency and emissions standards for stoves sold in EU
- **Medium Combustion Plant Directive** — affects commercial installations
- **National clean air plans** — many municipalities banning old stoves, replacing with modern ones that need proper draft

### 3. Energy Price Volatility
Post-2022 energy crisis: firewood is the cheapest heating option in most of Northern Europe. More people are using their stoves more intensively.

### 4. New Build Specifications
Architects increasingly specify wood-burning stoves as design elements in premium homes. These homes also specify smart home systems — draft fans need to integrate.
