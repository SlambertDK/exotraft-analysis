# Exodraft — Macro Trends & Regulatory Tailwinds

## 1. The Heat Pump Paradox
The EU's push for heat pumps is paradoxically increasing wood stove usage:
- Heat pumps replace gas/oil as primary heat source
- Wood stoves retained as backup/supplementary source
- But now the stove is used MORE INTENSIVELY during cold snaps when heat pumps underperform
- Draft problems that were tolerable when stove was used 5x/year are unacceptable when used 50x/year
- **Implication:** Heat pump rollout = larger Exodraft market

## 2. EU EcoDesign Directive for Solid Fuel Heaters
- **Regulation (EU) 2015/1185 and 2015/1189** — efficiency and emissions limits
- **2022 update** — tightened PM2.5 and NOx limits for new stoves
- Older stoves (pre-2022) produce 3-5x more particulates than new EcoDesign stoves
- Many municipalities incentivizing/mandating replacement of old stoves
- New EcoDesign stoves require better draft for optimal combustion — creates Exodraft demand
- **Implication:** Regulatory upgrade cycle = forced replacement market

## 3. Urban Air Quality Crisis
- Wood smoke is now the #1 source of PM2.5 in many European cities in winter
- Copenhagen, Oslo, Stockholm all have winter wood-burning alerts
- Insurance companies beginning to price chimney fire risk more accurately
- Municipal programs subsidizing clean combustion upgrades
- **Implication:** Exodraft becomes an air quality solution, not just a convenience product

## 4. Smart Home Integration Wave
- Matter standard (2022+) unifying smart home ecosystem
- Apple Home, Google Home, Amazon Alexa all expanding to HVAC
- Consumers expect every home device to be connected
- Premium smart home buyers (Exodraft's core customer) expect app control
- **Implication:** Connectivity is table stakes within 3 years — Exodraft must lead now

## 5. Energy Price Volatility (Post-2022)
- Russian gas cut-off tripled gas/oil prices 2022-2023
- Firewood is now 50-70% cheaper per kWh than gas in most of Northern Europe
- Wood stove usage up 40%+ in Denmark, Sweden, Norway, Germany 2022-2024
- New wood stove sales at record highs
- **Implication:** More stoves = more draft problems = more Exodraft customers

## 6. Airbnb / Short-Term Rental Boom
- Hosts with fireplaces command 20-30% premium on Airbnb
- Bad draft = negative reviews = revenue loss
- Property managers managing 5-50 properties need reliable, remote-monitored solutions
- **Implication:** B2B2C channel through Airbnb/Booking.com partnerships

## 7. Restaurant / Hospitality Trend
- Wood-fired pizza, meat, and bread is a global culinary trend
- Every premium restaurant in Northern Europe is installing wood-fired ovens
- Commercial draft problems are more expensive (lost revenue, health inspections)
- **Implication:** Commercial segment = premium pricing, high urgency, B2B procurement
