# Exodraft — Top Talking Points for Henrik's Meeting

*These are the 5 angles most likely to spark strategic conversation with the CEO.*

---

## 1. 🔥 "The Fan is the Trojan Horse"

The real product is combustion intelligence. The fan collects data every time it runs — draft pressure, temperature, resistance changes, run time. At 10,000 connected units, Exodraft has the world's largest dataset on real-world chimney performance.

**The question to ask:** "What happens to your company valuation when you have 10,000 connected units generating real-time combustion data — and Tryg, Topdanmark, or a municipality wants to buy access to it?"

---

## 2. 🔥 "Regulatory Tailwind Is Worth More Than Any Sales Team"

EU EcoDesign 2022 for solid fuel heaters mandates efficiency and emissions standards. Old stoves must be replaced. New stoves require better draft for optimal combustion. Exodraft doesn't need to convince people they have a problem — regulation is doing that.

**The question to ask:** "Is Exodraft positioned as the recommended solution when municipalities or insurance companies tell homeowners to upgrade?"

---

## 3. 🔥 "The Installer Channel Is Winner-Take-All"

Chimney sweeps do 750,000+ annual service visits in Denmark alone. They are trusted, in the home, and recommend products. If Exodraft becomes the default recommendation from Danish sweeps, Enervex cannot compete — not on product, not on price.

**The question to ask:** "How many certified Exodraft installers exist today? What would it cost to get 200 sweeps recommending Exodraft to 10 customers per year?"

---

## 4. 💡 "Germany Is the Prize"

Denmark validates the model. Germany scales it. 11 million wood-burning installations, the strictest air quality laws in Europe, a culture that values engineering quality, and no dominant smart chimney fan player. One German HVAC distributor with Exodraft in their catalog changes the company's trajectory.

**The question to ask:** "What is the plan for Germany — and does it start with a distributor, a direct team, or a white-label partnership?"

---

## 5. 💡 "Fan-as-a-Service Removes the #1 Objection"

The price objection ("€500 for a chimney fan?") disappears at €9/month. FaaS converts a hardware company into a recurring revenue business, improves LTV, and makes the unit economics visible to investors. Enervex cannot do this without reinventing their business model.

**The question to ask:** "Has Exodraft modeled what the business looks like at 5,000 FaaS subscribers vs. 5,000 hardware sales?"

---

## Bonus: The Exit Story

Three obvious acquirers:
- **Systemair** (owns Exhausto) — wants the connected product they never built
- **Wienerberger/Schiedel** — wants smart product for premium chimney systems
- **Insurance/energy conglomerate** — wants the combustion data platform

If Exodraft builds to €5M ARR with 40%+ recurring revenue and a defensible data layer, the exit multiple is 6-10x ARR = €30-50M. At €10M ARR, €60-100M.

**The question to ask:** "Is this a lifestyle business or an exit play — and does the strategy match that answer?"
