# Exodraft — Top Talking Points for Henrik's Meeting

*These are the 6 conversation angles most likely to spark strategic dialogue with Exodraft leadership.*
*Written for a meeting with an **established 67-year market leader** — not a startup pitch.*

---

## 1. 🔥 "You Have a Hardware Company. You Could Have a Platform."

**The hook:** Exodraft's chimney fans are on hundreds of thousands of chimneys across Europe. Each one is a sensor waiting to be turned on. Right now that data disappears. It doesn't have to.

**Why it resonates:** Leadership at an established company understands moats. A data platform is a moat that no Chinese hardware competitor can copy in 18 months.

**The question to leave them with:**
> "If every Exodraft fan sent you anonymized draft pressure and flue temperature data every minute — what would you know about your customers that you don't know today? And what would you be able to sell that you can't sell today?"

**What Henrik brings:** He's built connected product thinking into MaxBuddy from day one. He understands platform architecture, not just product features.

---

## 2. 🔥 "The Installation Barrier Is Your Biggest Growth Lever"

**The hook:** A €400 Exodraft fan becomes a €1,200+ installed solution. That gap kills 50-70% of interested buyers before they ever reach a dealer. This isn't a product problem — it's a go-to-market problem.

**Why it resonates:** Exodraft has a dealer/installer channel. But the channel has friction. Expanding the self-install addressable market triples the TAM without touching B2B at all.

**The question to leave them with:**
> "If a homeowner could install an Exodraft fan themselves — no electrician, no roofer, 20 minutes — what happens to your residential conversion rate? And what happens to the competitive picture when Chinese players try to do the same?"

**What Henrik brings:** Identified solar-powered, pole-mount, zero-cable as the solution path. Done the BOM work. This isn't a vague idea — it's a specced concept ready for engineering validation.

---

## 3. 🔥 "Regulation is Your Sales Team — Are You Ready?"

**The hook:** EU EcoDesign directives, German BImSchV clean air laws, UK Clean Air Act, and Nordic municipal regulations are all tightening. Old stoves will be forced out. New stoves need better combustion. Exodraft is in every conversation — but only if they're positioned to be.

**Why it resonates:** Exodraft already lobbies and influences policy. They know this. The question is whether they're converting regulatory pressure into sales pipeline systematically.

**The question to leave them with:**
> "When a homeowner in Stuttgart gets a letter saying their old stove doesn't comply, and they Googgle 'how do I make my chimney cleaner' — does Exodraft appear? And when they call an installer, does that installer recommend Exodraft by default?"

**What Henrik brings:** An AI content agent can publish and maintain SEO content at scale. A regulatory monitoring agent can flag new legislation before competitors act. This is doable in 90 days.

---

## 4. 💡 "Germany Is the Endgame. Is There a Plan?"

**The hook:** Germany has 11 million wood-burning installations, the strictest air quality regulations in Europe, a culture that values engineering quality and "Made in Denmark/EU" credentials, and no dominant smart chimney fan player. One national distributor relationship changes Exodraft's trajectory.

**Why it resonates:** Exodraft is already in markets beyond Denmark (UK case studies visible on website). Germany is the logical next scale market. But scaling into Germany traditionally requires a sales team, marketing budget, dealer recruitment — all expensive and slow.

**The question to leave them with:**
> "What would a Germany entry look like if you led with a connected product that doesn't require a local sales team on day one — because the product sells itself through content, regulation compliance messaging, and installer referral?"

**What Henrik brings:** AI-driven market entry strategy. Lead generation agents that map German chimney sweep associations and generate outreach. Content engines that publish German-language articles targeting regulation-driven search queries.

---

## 5. 💡 "Xzense Is the Foundation — But It's Not There Yet"

**The hook:** Exodraft has already made the first move toward connectivity with Xzense. That's valuable — they're not starting from zero. But "remote control" and "connected intelligence" are two very different products. The gap between them is where the opportunity lives.

**Why it resonates:** Leadership knows Xzense. They invested in it. The conversation isn't "you should build a connected product" — it's "here's the next three steps from where you already are."

**The question to leave them with:**
> "What would Xzense need to become in order for a homeowner to say 'my Exodraft fan knows my chimney better than I do'? And what data does it already have access to that you're not capturing?"

**What Henrik brings:** Concrete product roadmap from remote control → smart controller → AI combustion advisor. He's done this thinking. He has the architecture.

---

## 6. 💡 "Your Biggest Risk Isn't a Competitor. It's Irrelevance."

**The hook:** Exodraft's competitive moat today is brand trust, installer relationships, and product range. These are real and defensible. But they defend against traditional competitors. They don't defend against a well-funded hardware startup that launches a solar-powered, app-connected, self-install chimney fan at €249 on Amazon — and grows to 100,000 European users in 18 months.

**Why it resonates:** This is the innovator's dilemma framing, but specific. Not hypothetical. The components to build this product exist today. Someone will do it. The question is whether it's Exodraft or someone else.

**The question to leave them with:**
> "If you were starting Exodraft today — knowing what you know about the market, the regulatory tailwinds, and what AI and connected hardware can now do — what would the product look like? And why isn't that the product you're building?"

**What Henrik brings:** He doesn't just ask the question. He comes with the answer — a specced concept, a product roadmap, and an AI acceleration plan. Not a consultant who leaves them with slides. A thinking partner who leaves them with a plan.
