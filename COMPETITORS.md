# Exodraft — Competitive Landscape
*Research prepared for Henrik Lambert's advisory engagement — March 2026*

---

## 1. Competitive Position Overview

Exodraft is not a challenger or a startup in this market. It *is* the market. The company's origin is the invention of the product category — Kai Hermansen created the first commercially viable chimney fan in 1957. This heritage shapes the competitive landscape fundamentally: Exodraft defines the standard that others follow or approximate.

However, market leadership is never permanent. Three distinct threat vectors exist:
1. **Commoditization from below** (Chinese OEM hardware)
2. **Adjacent encroachment** (HVAC/chimney system companies expanding into fans)
3. **Platform displacement** (someone builds connected chimney products first)

## 2. Direct Competitors

### 2.1 Enervex (US-based, Atlanta)
**Website:** enervex.com
**Ownership:** Independent / Private
**Founded:** ~1980s (as Exhausto US, later rebranded Enervex)

**Background:**
Enervex is the most direct global competitor to Exodraft. In fact, Enervex *was* Exhausto's US operation — the brands share the same product DNA from the original chimney fan patents. When Exhausto reorganized into Exodraft, the US entity separated into Enervex.

**Product range:**
- ECCO Fan: residential chimney fan, top-mounted
- AD Series: industrial draft fans
- FBR Series: fireplace fans
- RS Series (similar naming to Exodraft — shared heritage)
- Control systems and accessories

**Geographic focus:**
- Primarily North America
- Some European presence (limited vs. Exodraft)

**Strengths:**
- Identical technical heritage to Exodraft
- Strong in North American market where Exodraft has limited presence
- Respectable product quality

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited European distribution versus Exodraft
- No clear connected/smart product roadmap evident
- Smaller product range than Exodraft's current portfolio
- No particle filter equivalent
- No group structure (climate, energy divisions)

**Competitive threat level:** **Medium** — real in North America, limited in Exodraft's core European markets. Could become more aggressive if North American market demands European expansion.

### 2.2 Systemair AB (Swedish HVAC Giant)
**Website:** systemair.com
**HQ:** Skinnskatteberg, Sweden
**Founded:** 1974
**Revenue:** ~SEK 10B+ (large HVAC company)
**Listed:** Nasdaq Stockholm

**Background:**
Systemair is a major pan-European ventilation and HVAC company. They produce fans, air handling units, heat exchangers, and building ventilation systems. Some of their product lines — particularly industrial fans and heat exchangers — overlap with Exodraft's territory.

**Relevant products:**
- Roof fans and exhaust ventilation products
- Industrial fans
- Heat recovery ventilation systems

**Competitive relevance:**
Systemair competes at the *systems* level — they sell full HVAC installations to building contractors. Chimney fans are a small, specialist niche within their vast product catalog. However:
- They have distribution in all Exodraft's key markets
- They have engineering resources to develop a targeted chimney fan product
- Their heat recovery solutions overlap with Exodraft's industrial offerings

**Strengths:**
- Scale, financial resources
- European distribution already established
- Engineering depth

**Weaknesses:**
- Chimney fans are a niche — would not receive strategic priority
- Lacks 67 years of chimney fan expertise
- No particle filter / combustion optimization heritage

**Competitive threat level:** **Low-Medium** — not a focused threat today, but has the resources to become one if they chose to prioritize this segment. Acquisition target? (Exodraft acquiring small Systemair product lines, or vice versa.)

### 2.3 Schiedel GmbH (Germany — Chimney Systems)
**Website:** schiedel.com
**HQ:** Munich, Germany
**Ownership:** Part of the Monier / BMI Group

**Background:**
Schiedel is Europe's leading chimney system manufacturer — prefabricated chimney systems, liners, flue systems. They are a natural adjacent competitor: they sell the chimney; adding a fan to it is an obvious extension.

**Competitive relevance:**
Schiedel sells to the same installer community as Exodraft. Their chimney installer already trusts them; if Schiedel launched a fan product, it would enter through that trusted relationship.

**Current position:** Schiedel's primary business is chimney structure, not active devices. They do not appear to have a mature chimney fan product range competing directly with Exodraft.

**Strengths:**
- Dominant chimney system brand in Germany
- Same installer touchpoint as Exodraft
- Massive distribution across Europe

**Weaknesses:**
- Expertise in passive chimney systems, not active fan technology
- Would need acquisition or licensing to match Exodraft's fan range
- No combustion optimization or data heritage

**Competitive threat level:** **Low (today), Medium-High (if they acquire a fan company)**. Schiedel could become a serious threat through an acquisition. They are worth monitoring as a potential acquirer of smaller fan companies — or potentially a target for Exodraft partnership/OEM deal.

### 2.4 Wamsler (German stove manufacturer)
**Website:** wamsler.de
**HQ:** Munich, Germany

Wamsler manufactures wood-burning stoves and cooking stoves. They are a complementary/adjacent player, not a direct competitor. However, stove manufacturers are an emerging threat vector: if Wamsler decided to integrate a proprietary chimney fan into their stoves as an OEM feature, it would close a distribution door for Exodraft.

**Competitive threat level:** **Low as competitor, medium as OEM opportunity or platform threat**. If Wamsler built its own connected stove ecosystem, Exodraft could be locked out of that brand's install base.

---

### 2.5 Chinese OEM Manufacturers (Amazon/Alibaba)
**Platforms:** Amazon.co.uk, .de, .fr / Alibaba / AliExpress
**Key brands:** Unnamed; various "chimney draft fan" products

**Background:**
Chinese manufacturers have entered the chimney fan market at the low end. Products are available on Amazon Europe for €80–€200, compared to Exodraft's €250–600 range. Quality is variable but improving.

**Current reality:**
- Chinese fans lack temperature tolerance certification for demanding applications
- Poor after-sales support and no installer relationship
- Limited warranty and unknown longevity
- Not certified to European safety standards in many cases (CE marking self-declared)
- No particle filter integration

**The trajectory — this is the real threat:**
In 2020, these products were barely functional. By 2025, some have improved meaningfully. By 2028–2030:
- Quality will approach "good enough" for basic residential use
- Price will be 40–60% below Exodraft
- Amazon reviews will route basic residential buyers away from Exodraft
- The €300 "simple draft fan" market segment will commoditize

**What this means:**
Exodraft needs to be *clearly above* the commodity tier in 3 years. That means:
- AI/connectivity features that Chinese OEMs cannot match
- Data services that create switching costs
- Regulatory certification depth that takes years to build
- Professional installer relationships that pure-hardware players cannot replicate

**Competitive threat level:** **High (3–5 year horizon)** — not an immediate crisis but the most structurally important threat to Exodraft's residential market.

## 3. The White Space: No Connected Competitor

This is perhaps the most strategically important observation:

**No chimney fan company has built a genuinely connected, AI-powered product platform.**

- Enervex: no evidence of smart products
- Systemair: smart products in HVAC, not in chimney fans
- Schiedel: passive products only
- Chinese OEMs: basic timer controls at best
- Exodraft: **Xzense exists** but appears to be remote control / basic automation, not a true data platform

The company that builds this first — connected fans, combustion data, fleet learning, AI coaching, smart home integration — will own the premium segment of this market for the next decade.

Exodraft has every advantage to be first: brand trust, installer access, 67 years of engineering knowledge, and a development lab. The question is whether they move before someone else does.

## 4. Competitive Moats — Exodraft's Defensible Position

| Moat | Description | Durability |
|------|-------------|------------|
| **Brand heritage (67 years)** | Kai Hermansen invented the product. The brand is the market. | Very high — reputational moats compound over time |
| **Installer relationships** | Thousands of certified dealers across Northern Europe trust Exodraft products | High — these relationships took decades to build |
| **Development laboratory** | Proprietary R&D facility; enables temperature testing, compliance verification | High — not replicable overnight |
| **Patent history** | Chimney fan patents from inception; ongoing product patents | Medium — hardware patents erode; software/AI patents are new battleground |
| **Regulatory presence** | Active engagement with policy-makers; their data shapes regulations | Medium-High — depends on sustained investment |
| **Temperature expertise** | RSHT at 700°C; nobody else does this at commercial scale | High — narrow but genuinely differentiated |
| **Full product range** | Residential to industrial; residential fan to full heat recovery system | Medium — could be matched by a large HVAC company |
| **Group structure** | Climate + Energy divisions create cross-sell opportunity | Medium — depends on integration execution |

## 6. Acquisition / Partnership Landscape

**Potential targets for Exodraft to acquire:**
- A software/IoT platform company in home energy management
- A chimney sweep management software platform (schedule, digitize sweeps)
- A combustion sensor startup

**Potential acquirers of Exodraft:**
- Systemair (if they wanted chimney fan leadership)
- Nibe (Swedish heat pump giant looking at adjacent categories)
- A private equity roll-up of European energy tech companies

**Potential OEM/partnership deals:**
- Jøtul (Norwegian stove manufacturer — premium)
- Rais (Danish, design-focused premium stoves)
- Morsø (Danish, strong brand in DK/UK)
- Hwam (Danish stoves with existing smart technology — interesting synergy)
- Contura (Swedish premium stoves)
