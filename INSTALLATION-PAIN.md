# Exodraft — Installation Friction: The #1 Customer Acquisition Problem
*Research prepared for Henrik Lambert's advisory engagement — March 2026*

---

## 1. The Problem in One Number

**€400 product cost → €1,200+ total installed cost**

For most residential buyers, this is the conversion killer. The Exodraft product is good. The value proposition (15% fuel savings, cleaner burning, better draft) is compelling. But the total cost of ownership conversation starts with "you'll need a roofer and an electrician" — and approximately 50–70% of interested buyers stop there.

This is not a marketing problem. It is a product-market-fit problem at the distribution layer.

---

## 2. What Installation Actually Involves

### Step 1: Physical Access to the Chimney Top
All of Exodraft's residential top-mounted fans (EFC range, RSV, Draftbooster) require installation at the chimney termination point — typically at the apex of the roof.

**The challenge:**
- Most homes do not have permanent roof access
- Rooftop work is:
  - Physically demanding (steep pitches, weather exposure)
  - Safety-regulated (working at height requirements in all EU markets)
  - Insurance-restricted (many installers won't do rooftop work without specialist scaffolding or cherry picker)
- A roofer or specialist installer visit: **€150–400 depending on access complexity**
- In some cases, scaffolding is required: **€300–800 additional**

**Who can legitimately install?**
- Trained HVAC installers (Exodraft certified dealers) — but most don't specialize in rooftop access
- Chimney sweeps — already on the roof, legally and practically. **This is the underutilized channel.**
- Specialist roofers (limited cross-referral to chimney fan sales)

---

### Step 2: Electrical Connection
A chimney fan consumes 40–60W. It needs a power supply. For top-mounted fans, this means:
- Running a cable from the fan (chimney top) to the nearest power source
- Options: down the inside of the chimney, externally down the chimney structure, or through the roof
- Requires: a licensed electrician to make the final connection and sign off
- In many EU countries: building permit or notification for new electrical circuits in the roof

**Cost of electrical connection:**
- Simple interior route (cable through loft, short run): **€100–200**
- Complex route (external, long run, permit required): **€300–600**
- Average: **€200–300**

**Timeline:** Electrician availability can add 2–6 weeks to the installation timeline. This is a conversion killer — motivated buyers cool off while waiting for trade availability.

---

### Step 3: Combined Cost Reality

| Component | Low Estimate | High Estimate | Typical |
|-----------|:-----------:|:-------------:|:-------:|
| Exodraft EFC18 (product) | €320 | €380 | €350 |
| Dealer markup (25–40%) | €80 | €150 | €120 |
| Rooftop access (installer + safety) | €150 | €400 | €250 |
| Electrical connection (cable + electrician) | €100 | €600 | €250 |
| Scaffolding (if required) | €0 | €800 | €100 (avg) |
| **Total installed cost** | **€650** | **€2,330** | **€1,070** |

For the median residential buyer, the total cost is approximately **3x the product cost**. This is the core problem.

## 3. The Buyer Psychology

### The "ask the sweep" moment
Most residential buyers first consider a chimney fan because of a problem: smoky startup, poor draft, smell in the room. They ask their chimney sweep. If the sweep says "yes, a fan would help, it's about €400" — the buyer says yes. When the sweep says "you'll also need an electrician and possibly scaffolding" — the buyer says "let me think about it."

They rarely come back. Life intervenes. The pain subsides. The purchase never happens.

### The online research loop
A buyer who researches independently:
1. Finds the Exodraft website or a dealer
2. Sees the product at €300–400
3. Contacts dealer about installation
4. Receives quote including labor: €900–1,400 total
5. Googles "chimney fan cheaper option" → finds Amazon listings at €80–150

The €80 Amazon fan is a false economy (quality, certification, warranty issues) — but the buyer doesn't know that, and the price comparison at Step 5 kills premium product consideration.

## 4. What Exodraft Already Does to Mitigate This

### 4.1 The Draftbooster — A Partial Solution
The **Draftbooster** is Exodraft's residential product positioned for easier installation. Key features that reduce friction:
- Compact, lighter form factor than the full EFC range
- Designed for easier chimney-top fitting
- Marketed as accessible to the homeowner-plus-sweep scenario

**However:** The Draftbooster still requires electrical connection. It is not fully self-install. It reduces the physical installation challenge but does not eliminate the electrician requirement.

**The Draftbooster is the right product direction — it just needs to go further.**

---

### 4.2 Certified Dealer Network
Exodraft's certified dealer network is designed to provide turnkey installation (product + labor in one quote). This works well when:
- The dealer is geographically accessible
- The dealer has spare capacity
- The dealer's labor pricing is reasonable

It fails when:
- No dealer is nearby (rural areas, underserved regions)
- Dealer quote is too high relative to product cost
- Customer wants to self-install but can't without an electrician

---

### 4.3 EBC Series for Base-Mounted Applications
The **EBC24, EBC22, and EBC10v2** are designed for boiler applications — mounted at the boiler, not at the chimney top. For oil and gas boiler applications, this eliminates the rooftop access problem entirely.

This is an undermarketed insight: **for boiler applications, the installation barrier is significantly lower.** The product is installed in the utility room or plant room, accessible at floor level. Electrical connection is straightforward.

If Exodraft developed a base-mounted or mid-chimney fan for wood-burning stoves (not just boilers), it would dramatically reduce the installation barrier for a new residential segment.

## 5. The Solutions — Mapped to Exodraft's Specific Context

### Solution A: Solar-Powered Self-Install Product (New Product)
**Impact level: ★★★★★ (transformative)**

A solar-powered chimney fan eliminates the electrical connection requirement entirely:
- Integrated solar panel on the fan housing powers the motor
- LiFePO4 battery provides overnight and cloudy-day operation
- Thermoelectric generator (TEG) on the flue collar provides supplemental power from heat

**What self-install looks like with solar:**
1. Homeowner carries unit up a ladder (lightweight, <3 kg)
2. Snap-mount onto existing chimney cap (magnetic or friction fit)
3. Download app, pair via BLE
4. Done — no electrician, no permit, no cable

**Who installs it in practice:**
- The chimney sweep (30 minutes, adds €50–100 to a sweep visit)
- The homeowner (60 minutes, ladder required)
- A handyman (no electrical certification required)

**Economic impact:**
Current: €350 product + €700 labor = €1,050 total → marginal conversion
Solar: €299–349 product + €0–100 sweep install = €299–449 total → high conversion

This is a 3x addressable market expansion. Not an incremental improvement — a category redefinition.

### Solution B: Chimney Sweep Channel Expansion (Channel Strategy)
**Impact level: ★★★★ (significant)**

Chimney sweeps are the ideal installation partner:
- Already on the roof (rooftop access is their routine job)
- Already trusted by homeowners (regulatory requirement = captive relationship)
- Visit every chimney annually (in Germany, legally mandated)
- Need to differentiate their service offering (fan install = premium service = higher revenue)

**Why this channel is underutilized:**
- Sweeps need training to recommend and install correctly
- Sweeps need a margin structure that makes it worth their time
- Sweeps need a simple installation (hence: solar-powered fan, no electrician needed)

**The Exodraft sweep program should include:**
1. Free certification training (1 day, Langeskov or online)
2. Dealer pricing with minimum 40% margin on product
3. Installation guide optimized for sweep workflow
4. Lead generation support (Exodraft sends nearby leads to certified sweeps)
5. Co-branded marketing (sweep recommends "Exodraft-certified" fan)

**Scale potential:**
- Germany alone has ~10,000 registered chimney sweeps, each visiting 400–800 homes annually
- Even 10% conversion = 400,000 additional sales conversations per year in Germany alone
- This is the distribution unlock that makes the chimney fan a mass-market product

---

### Solution C: "Soft-Mount" Pole Installation (Product Extension)
**Impact level: ★★★ (good)**

For chimneys where the chimney pot is accessible from a window, dormer, or flat roof section — a pole-mounted fan on an extension pole (M16 thread, compatible with sweep rods) could be installed from a window without rooftop access.

The fan sits on an extension pole lowered into the chimney flue:
- No rooftop access required
- No electrical cable to run (solar-powered)
- Installed in under 15 minutes

This serves the "impossible chimney" market: old houses, converted buildings, urban apartments where rooftop access is prohibitively expensive or physically impossible.

---

### Solution D: Base-Mounted Wood Stove Fan (New Product Category)
**Impact level: ★★★ (niche but important)**

For homes with masonry fireplaces (no external chimney pot), or chimneys where the fan cannot be top-mounted:
- A draft-inducing device mounted at the stove collar or stovepipe connection
- Creates draft by accelerating gas at the stove outlet, not the chimney top
- Less efficient than a top-mounted fan (less draft pressure differential) but functional

Exodraft already makes base-mounted products for boilers (EBC range). Extending this technology to wood-burning stoves opens the market of homes that currently cannot use any Exodraft product.

---

### Solution E: "One Quote" Installer Partnership (Process Innovation)
**Impact level: ★★★ (short-term impact)**

Without changing the product, Exodraft can reduce friction through process:
- Partner with a national scaffolding/cherry picker company to offer bundled quotes
- Create a nationwide installer map with real-time availability and online booking
- Offer "online quote in 60 seconds" calculator (chimney height + type → instant installed price)

The goal: eliminate the friction of multiple calls and uncertain pricing. A buyer who gets a single, transparent, guaranteed price within 5 minutes of visiting the website converts at 2–3x the rate of one who must call around.

---

### Solution F: DIY Subsection — Self-Install Products Listed on Website
**Impact level: ★★ (incremental)**

Exodraft's website currently routes residential buyers to a dealer. But some buyers (rental properties, remote locations, EU countries with no nearby dealer) will never find a dealer.

A self-install product tier sold direct-to-consumer — with a comprehensive installation video, warranty coverage for self-install, and a support hotline — would capture buyers currently lost to Amazon alternatives.

This is not ideal (Exodraft's installer network is an asset, not a liability) but it is better than losing those buyers to Chinese OEM products.
