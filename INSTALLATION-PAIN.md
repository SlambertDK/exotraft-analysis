# Exodraft — Installation Pain Analysis & Solutions

## The Two Adoption Killers

These are not minor UX issues. They are conversion killers that cost Exodraft 50-70% of interested buyers.

### Pain 1: Chimney top mounting
- Ladder access = dangerous or impossible for most homeowners
- Requires roofer OR chimney sweep with roof access
- Two trades = double scheduling, double cost

### Pain 2: Getting power to chimney top
- Most chimneys have zero electrical infrastructure
- Running cable: 3-6 hours of electrician work = €400-800
- Permits required in many municipalities
- Creates a second scheduling dependency on top of the installer

**Combined effect:** A €400 fan becomes a €1,200 installed solution. Conversion drops dramatically.

---

## Solving Mounting

### Option A: Ground-level installation (radical)
Mount at chimney base, not top. Push from bottom instead of pull from top.
- Zero ladder access needed
- Zero roofer required
- Less aerodynamically efficient but solvable with engineering
- Precedent: Inline duct fans in HVAC work exactly this way

### Option B: Magnetic snap-mount system
Integrated chimney cap + fan unit. Existing cap comes off, Exodraft snaps on.
- No tools, no drilling
- Homeowner installs in 10 minutes from roof edge with extension pole
- Sweep installs during annual service visit

### Option C: Extension pole installation
Explicitly designed for pole-mount from ground level.
- No ladder, no roofer
- Every chimney sweep already owns telescoping rods
- Sweep becomes natural installer without needing roof access

### Option D: Drone installation (3-5 year horizon)
Commercial drones can place objects precisely at chimney height.
- Zero human roof access
- Urban areas: drone service as premium installation option
- Exodraft partners with drone delivery company

## Solving Power (The Bigger Problem)

### Solution 1: 🔥 Solar-powered (Highest priority)
Integrate solar panel directly into fan unit. Battery backup for evening/night fires.

- A chimney fan draws 15-40W
- 20W solar panel + 100Wh battery = fully off-grid for typical use (2-4 hour fires)
- Technology exists today (BioLite does this for camping stoves)
- **No electrician. No cable. No permit.**
- Market precedent: solar-powered garden lights, cameras, weather stations

### Solution 2: 🔥 Thermoelectric Generation (TEG)
The chimney flue reaches 150-300°C during operation. TEG collar converts heat differential to electricity.

- Fan powers *itself* from the chimney's own heat
- Zero external power dependency
- Zero solar dependency (works at night, works in winter)
- TEG efficiency: 5-8% — achievable for this application
- Companies making TEGs: Alphabet Energy, KELK, Micropelt
- **No one in chimney fans has done this. First-mover territory.**

### Solution 3: 💡 Battery + inductive charging
Fan runs on large rechargeable battery (DeWalt/Makita power tool format).
Charges via inductive pad mounted at reachable height inside chimney breast.

- No cable up the chimney
- Short cable inside the house only = no permit, 30 min electrician
- Battery: 8-12 hours operation per charge
- Swap battery while charging — never miss a fire

### Solution 4: 💡 Thin-film cable through chimney liner
2mm flat heat-resistant cable runs down inside of chimney liner.
Exits at stove connection point inside the house.

- No roofer, no external cable
- Heat-resistant cable exists (industrial applications)
- DIY-installable
- Electrician only connects indoor end: 30 min, no permits

### Solution 5: 💡 Power-over-liner (new builds)
Cable channel integrated into stainless steel flexible liner.
New builds get power infrastructure "for free" during liner install.

- Partner with Schiedel, Poujoulat, Jeremias for "Exodraft-ready liners"
- Zero additional installation cost for new installations

## The Killer Product Concept: Exodraft Solar

**One product that eliminates both pains simultaneously:**

> Integrated solar panel + TEG backup + battery.
> Magnetic snap-mount onto existing chimney pot.
> Self-installable with extension pole.
> App-connected via Bluetooth.
> Zero electrician. Zero roofer. Zero permit.

**Installation experience:**
1. Order online
2. Attach magnetic base adapter to chimney pot (sweep does during annual visit, or pole from ground)
3. Snap fan unit onto base
4. Download app, connect
5. **Done. 20 minutes total.**

**This product does not exist anywhere in the market today.**
